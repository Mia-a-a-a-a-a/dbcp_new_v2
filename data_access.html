Connecting you to the ocean: fast, reliable, and open access to buoy data

Access to buoy data

Real-time

Buoy data is generally available in real time to platform operators via telecommunications providers and very quickly distributed on the Global Telecommunications System of the WMO (GTS) and the WMO Information System (WIS2.0).

  • What is a GTS
  • Graphical views of GTS data for 16 days, for active buoys, by MeteoFrance
  • The WMO Information System WIS2.0

Near-real time and Archived data

  • Canada's Integrated Science Data management center provides an archive of all buoy data on behalf of the DBCP and GOOS. Data coming from the GTS is usually available via the ISDM web site within 2 months of observation.
  • The USA National Oceanographic Data center (NODC) provides an archive of a subset of buoy data and the US National Climate Data Center (NCDC) provides an archive of surface data.
  • Coriolis in France provides access to most buoy data on the GTS
